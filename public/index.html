📖 METAR GO — User Guide
v4.3.6 · Training Edition · Quick reference for all features
🆕 What's New
💡 This popup shows once on each update. You can always review it here.
🌦 WEATHER Tab (Dashboard Mode)
What is the Weather Tab?
When Dashboard Mode is enabled in Settings, the separate METAR and TAF tabs are replaced by a single Weather tab. It combines both into one view, switchable via a pill toggle at the top.
METAR / TAF Pill Toggle
Tap METAR or TAF on the pill at the top of the Weather tab to switch panes. All the same data is shown — wind rose, sky cover, gauges, meteogram, TAF timeline — just in one unified tab.
Smart Default Tab
When Dashboard Mode is on and a default airport is set, the app opens directly to the Weather → METAR pane on launch. No manual navigation needed.
TAF Tap-to-Decode
In the Weather tab's TAF pane, tap any coloured forecast block on the timeline bar to expand the decoded detail card — showing wind, visibility, weather, and cloud for that period. Tap another block to switch periods.
Enabling / Disabling
Go to Settings → Multi-Airport Dashboard and toggle it on or off. When off, the original separate METAR and TAF tabs return.
🌤 METAR Tab
Wind Rose
Shows wind direction relative to the selected runway. Green = headwind, Red = tailwind. The runway selector auto-picks the best runway for current winds. CALM and VRB winds are labelled directly on the rose.
Flight Category Badge (top right)
VFR Ceiling >3000ft & Vis >5sm   MVFR 1000-3000ft / 3-5sm   IFR <1000ft / <3sm   LIFR <500ft / <1sm
Tap the badge to open the legend. Tap the time badge to toggle between Zulu and local airport time.
Trend Indicators (↗ ↘ →)
Appear next to Wind, Visibility, and Ceiling values after loading the same airport at least twice. Compares the current reading against ~1 hour ago. ↗ Better · ↘ Worse · → Steady
Relative Humidity (RH)
Shown in the footer below the raw METAR. Orange = dry (<30%), Blue = very humid (>80%). Temp–Dewpoint spread and RH together indicate fog risk.
Nearby Stations
Tap any chip to open a preview card showing METAR, flight category, distance, and elevation. Tap Load Full Data to switch to that airport. Distance and bearing (e.g. 23nm · 045° NE) are shown from your current station.
Raw METAR Highlights
Red = gusts / low ceiling   Purple = low visibility   Orange = thunderstorm / heavy precip
📋 Copy Button
Copies the raw METAR string to your clipboard for pasting into flight planning tools or dispatch messages.
🕐 Time Badge (top right)
The clock badge in the top bar always shows Zulu (UTC) time. Tap it to toggle to local airport time — the offset (e.g. UTC+8) is shown underneath. This requires an airport to be loaded and Open-Meteo data to be available. It is separate from the sunrise/sunset time toggle in the Info tab.
☁️ Sky Cover Visualization
The animated cloud icons in the Sky Cover card scale with actual coverage — FEW shows 2 icons, SCT shows 4, BKN shows 7, and OVC fills the row with 8. Layers are sorted highest to lowest. Only BKN, OVC, and VV (vertical visibility) count as ceiling layers for flight rules calculation.
📅 TAF Tab
Meteogram (24h Trend)
Shows temperature (red line), dewpoint (blue line), wind arrows, speed, and weather icons from Open-Meteo. The blue NOW line marks your current time. When the red and blue lines nearly converge, fog risk is elevated. Advisory only — not official aviation data.
TAF Timeline Bar
Each coloured block is a TAF forecast period. Tap a block to expand its details (wind, visibility, weather, clouds). The red needle marks current time — tap it to see UTC and local airport time. The thin strip below shows the day/night cycle.
TAF Expiry Timer
Shows time remaining on the current TAF. Turns orange when under 30 minutes remain. An ⚠️ EXPIRED badge appears if the TAF has lapsed.
NOTAMs
Colour-coded notices: ⛔ Critical (closures, unserviceable navaids), ⚠️ Caution (obstacles, work in progress), ℹ️ Info (general). Data from FAA aviationweather.gov — updated every 10 minutes.
🌩️ SIGMET / AIRMET
Displays active SIGMETs (severe weather — turbulence, icing, volcanic ash, thunderstorms) and AIRMETs (moderate-level hazards) for the loaded airport's region. US coverage via aviationweather.gov. For non-US airports, a link to the relevant national authority is shown. Tap ↻ to manually refresh. Timestamps show when data was last checked.
📋 Copy Button
Copies the full raw TAF text to clipboard.
ℹ️ INFO Tab
Pressure & Density Altitude
Pressure Alt = ISA-corrected altitude. Density Alt = performance altitude. A ⚠️ warning appears when DA is more than 2000ft above field elevation — expect degraded aircraft performance.
ISA Deviation
How far current temperature deviates from standard atmosphere. Positive = warmer (worse performance), Negative = cooler (better performance).
Sunrise / Sunset
Calculated from station coordinates using the NOAA algorithm. Defaults to Zulu. Tap the time to toggle UTC ↔ local airport time.
Frequencies
Loaded using a three-layer fallback: first from the built-in database (8,341 airports), then AVWX, then aviationweather.gov. Up to 9 frequencies displayed including ATIS, TWR, GND, APP, DEP, CTR, and UNICOM.
ATIS / AWOS Phone
Direct-dial phone numbers for Taiwan airports (RCTP, RCSS, RCKH, RCMQ, RCFN, RCBS, RCKU) are built-in. For other airports, a link to AirNav is provided. Tap a number to dial directly.
Runway Wind Components
Headwind ↑ / tailwind ↓ and crosswind ➡ / ⬅ for every runway. A ⚠️ LIMIT badge appears when crosswind exceeds your personal limit set in Tools.
📡 ATC Tab
CRAFT Clearance Recorder
Scratchpad for IFR clearances using the standard format: Clearance limit · Route · Altitude · Frequency · Transponder. Auto-saves as you type and persists between sessions.
Live Audio Streams
In-app ATIS and ATC scanner streams for Taiwan airports (RCTP, RCSS, RCKH, RCMQ, RCBS, RCFN) via TWATC.net. For all other airports, the LiveATC button searches by ICAO code.
External Links
Metar-Taf.com and LiveATC.net links auto-update to your currently loaded airport's ICAO code.
🚨 Emergency Squawk Codes
A quick-reference card is always visible at the bottom of the ATC tab:

7700 — General Emergency
7600 — Radio Failure (NORDO)
7500 — Unlawful Interference / Hijacking
1200 — VFR flight (United States)
2000 — Uncontrolled / no ATC instruction (International)

These codes are displayed at all times — no airport needs to be loaded.
🛠 TOOLS Tab
Cockpit Timer
Countdown timer for fuel burn or holding patterns. Presets: 15 / 30 / 45 / 60 min. The card flashes red and vibrates on expiry. Use PAUSE / RESUME to interrupt without resetting.
Personal Minimums
Enter your crosswind and ceiling limits. The app checks live METAR data and shows GO ✅ or NO-GO ⛔. A red banner also appears at the top of the screen. Limits are saved automatically.
✈️ Aviation Tools Collection
Tap Open Aviation Tools to launch the full tools overlay. Contains 10 tools: Unit Converter, E6B Flight Computer, E6B Trainer, Great Circle Distance, Crosswind Calculator, VFR Airspace Minimums, Weather Terms, Abbreviations, Morse Code Trainer, and 1:60 Rule. Use ← to go back to the tool menu, and ✕ to close the overlay entirely.
📏 Aviation Tools — Unit Converter
What units are supported?
10 categories: Distance (NM, SM, km, m, ft) · Altitude (ft, m, FL) · Speed (kt, mph, km/h, m/s, fpm) · Temperature (°C, °F, K) · Pressure (hPa, inHg, mb, PSI) · Fuel Volume (US gal, Imp gal, L, qt) · Weight (lbs, kg, tons, metric tons) · Fuel Flow (GPH, L/h, PPH) · Energy (J, kJ, MJ, Wh, kWh, BTU, ft·lb) · Power (W, kW, MW, hp, PS, BTU/h).
⇅ Swap Button
Tap the ⇅ button between FROM and TO to instantly flip the conversion direction — swaps both unit selectors and moves the result back as the new input value.
Fuel Flow (PPH) note
Pounds per Hour (PPH) conversions require fuel density and cannot be calculated directly. A note will appear when PPH is selected.
✈️ Aviation Tools — E6B Flight Computer
What does it calculate?
Enter Indicated Altitude, IAS, QNH, OAT, and Dewpoint to calculate: Density Altitude, True Airspeed (TAS), Estimated Cloud Base AGL, and Freezing Level. Supports hPa / inHg for QNH and °C / °F for temperature.
Density Altitude colour warning
The Density Altitude value turns orange when DA exceeds indicated altitude by more than 2,000 ft — a performance caution flag for hot / high conditions.
🌍 Aviation Tools — Great Circle Distance
How it works
Enter two ICAO airport codes to calculate the great circle distance (shortest path over the Earth's surface) and initial track bearing between them. Requires an airport coordinate database connection for live results.
🎓 Aviation Tools — E6B Trainer
What is the E6B Trainer?
An interactive E6B flight computer simulator from the University of North Dakota (UND). Loads in-app so you can practice wind correction, fuel burn, time/distance problems, and TAS calculations without leaving METAR GO.
🌦️ Aviation Tools — Weather Terms
What is shown?
A searchable reference of present weather codes used in METARs — including phenomena symbols (RA, SN, TS, FG, etc.), intensity prefixes, and descriptor modifiers. Tap any category chip to filter by type. Includes wind barb symbology with live-generated SVG examples.
Searching
Type any code or description into the search box to filter the list in real time. Tap ← Back to Main View to return to the full list after a category filter.
📖 Aviation Tools — Abbreviations
What is the Abbreviations tool?
An embedded searchable dictionary of aviation acronyms and abbreviations, powered by Simplify Acronym Search. Look up any ICAO, FAA, or general aviation abbreviation directly in-app without switching browsers.
🌬️ Aviation Tools — Crosswind Calculator
Two input modes
The calculator has two modes: Visual Compass — drag the wind arrow around the compass rose and adjust speed with the slider, or Type-In — enter wind direction, speed, gust, and runway heading numerically. Both produce the same output.
Output
Shows computed headwind/tailwind and crosswind components with direction (left/right). When gust is entered, worst-case gust components are also displayed. A ⚠️ LIMIT badge appears if crosswind exceeds your personal minimum set in Personal Minimums.
🛩️ Aviation Tools — VFR Airspace Minimums
What is shown?
A quick-reference card for FAA VFR weather minimums by airspace class (B, C, D, E, G) and altitude band. Shows required flight visibility and cloud clearance (above, below, horizontal) for each class. Useful for preflight planning and in-flight reference.
📡 Aviation Tools — Morse Code Trainer
Modes
Four training modes:

Learn — browse the full Morse alphabet with visual dot/dash display and audio playback per character.

Listen — hear a character played and identify it. Speed adjustable via WPM slider.

Quiz — given a character, tap the correct Morse pattern from multiple choices. Tracks your score.

Words — full word/sentence practice. Includes common VOR identifiers (e.g. TPE, LAX, SFO) for realistic navaid ident training.
Audio
Uses a 600 Hz sine tone generated in-browser. Speed is adjustable. Make sure your device is not on silent mode.
📐 Aviation Tools — Training Area
What is the Training Area?
A comprehensive training tool covering the 1:60 Rule and Wind Triangle — the essential mental-math skills every pilot needs. The 1:60 rule states that at small angles, 1° off course at 60 NM = 1 NM displacement.
Topics
Six topic tabs:

COURSE — Track Error (TE), Closing Angle (CA), Direct-to-Waypoint (TE+CA), and Double Track Error methods.

WIND — Crosswind/headwind components using sine/cosine shortcuts, Wind Correction Angle (WCA), and ground speed estimation.

TOD — Top of Descent distance, height-at-range checks, and the 300 ft/NM rule for 3° glideslopes.

V/S — Rate of descent from flight path angle (GS × 5 shortcut), gradient-to-FPA conversion, and constant-rate descent planning.

WIND △ — Interactive Wind Triangle with canvas visualisation. Three solve modes: find TT/GS, find TH/GS (default, ground school format with Variation → MH/MC), or find Wind.

ALT — Pressure Altitude, True Altitude, and Density Altitude calculations. Uses ISA at indicated altitude for True Alt and ISA at PA for Density Alt. Includes the 0.4%/°C air column correction and 120 ft/°C DA rule. ALT — Pressure Altitude, True Altitude (0.4%/°C air column correction), Density Altitude (PA + ISA dev × 120 ft/°C), and ISA deviation calculations.
Modes
Each topic has four modes: REF (formula reference cards), CALC (interactive calculator with live canvas for Wind Triangle), EXAMPLES (worked step-by-step problems), and QUIZ (randomised practice problems with scoring).
🌍 WORLD Tab
World Clock
Live local times with UTC offset. Type any ICAO code (e.g. EGLL, KJFK) and tap Add. Timezone is resolved automatically via coordinates. Tap ✕ to remove a city.
Cloud Sync of Cities
When Cloud Backup is active, you'll be asked whether to sync your city list. Once enabled, your world clock restores on any device using your Backup Code.
🗂 MULTI Tab (Dashboard)
Enabling the Dashboard
Go to Settings → Multi-Airport Dashboard and toggle it on. The MULTI tab appears in the navigation. Track up to 8 airports at once.
Adding Airports (ICAO or IATA)
Type either a 4-letter ICAO code (e.g. KJFK) or a 3-letter IATA code (e.g. JFK) into the add field and tap + Add. When an IATA code is entered the app automatically resolves it to the correct ICAO code and shows a brief confirmation toast (e.g. 🔍 JFK → KJFK). Both code types work identically once added.
Cards
Each card shows the raw METAR with colour-coded flight category and data age. Tap any card to load that airport's full data across all other tabs. The × button removes an airport from the dashboard.
Auto-Refresh
Data refreshes automatically every 5 minutes while the app is open. A ⚠️ badge appears on cards with data older than 60 minutes.
Cloud Sync
Your airport list is saved to Cloud Backup (if enabled) whenever you add or remove airports. It restores automatically on any device using your Backup Code.
⚙️ SETTINGS Tab
Favorite Airports ★
Appear as gold chips above the search bar for one-tap access. Synced to Cloud Backup when enabled. Add via Settings → Favorite Airports.
Default Airport
Loaded automatically every time the app opens. Set it once in Settings and the app always starts at your home airport.
Preferred Runway
After saving a Default Airport, a Preferred Runway selector appears beneath it. Choose a specific runway (e.g. RWY 05L) to lock the wind rose and runway component display to that runway on every launch — useful if your home airport has a noise-preferred or one-way runway. Set it to Auto to always have the app pick the optimal headwind runway from current METAR winds.
📐 Unit Preferences
Found in Settings → Unit Preferences. Three independent toggles:

Visibility — switch between statute miles (sm) and kilometres (km). Affects the Dashboard card and all visibility displays.

Temperature — switch between °C and °F. Affects Dashboard cards and the temp/dewpoint display.

Pressure — switch between hPa and inHg. Affects the altimeter/QNH display across all tabs.

Preferences are saved automatically and persist between sessions.
🎨 App Theme
Choose from 6 themes: Default dark, Cockpit Night (red-on-black for night vision), Sectional (warm VFR chart tones), IFR Enroute (navy), Phosphor (green CRT terminal), and High Contrast. Selection syncs to cloud backup.
☁️ Cloud Backup
Saves favorites, default airport, world clock cities, multi-airport list, app theme, and personal minimums using a 4–6 digit code. No account or personal data needed. Restore on any device with your code. Choose This Device Only if you prefer local-only storage.
API Usage Monitor
Admin-only view showing daily AVWX API call counts across all keys. Requires admin password. Useful for monitoring usage before plan limits are reached.
🔧 Admin Tool Control
Admins can hide/show individual tools for all users via the 🔧 Tools tab in the admin panel (in-app or admin.html). Hidden tools are invisible to normal users but always visible to the admin with a 👁 HIDDEN badge. Useful for staging new tools or restricting access during training.
Dashboard Card Style (Raw vs Detailed)
Visible in Settings only when Dashboard Mode is enabled. Controls how each airport card is displayed in the Dashboard tab:

Raw — shows the full METAR string with colour-highlighted gusts, low visibility, and weather phenomena. Best for experienced users who read raw METARs directly.

Detailed — decodes the METAR into a structured card showing wind direction arrow, speed, gust, visibility, cloud layer, temperature, dewpoint, RH, pressure, and data age. Also shows a TAF condition badge (initial → worst category) if a TAF is available. Best for quick at-a-glance status across multiple airports.
🆕 What's New Popup
On each app update, a popup shows once to highlight new features. Once dismissed it won't appear again for that version. You can always revisit the changelog here at the top of this Help tab.
🔍 Search, GPS & Navigation
Search Bar
Type any ICAO code (4 letters, e.g. RCTP, KJFK, EGLL) and press Enter or tap ↻. The field auto-capitalises. Recent airports appear as chips above the bar for quick re-loading.
📍 GPS Button
Uses your device GPS to find and load the nearest aviation weather station within 150nm. Distance and station name are shown in a toast notification. Allow location permission when prompted.
↻ Force Refresh
Clears the 10-minute data cache and fetches the latest METAR, TAF, and NOTAM data immediately from all sources.
Swipe Navigation
Swipe left to go to the next tab, right to go back. The tab bar and top bar stay fixed — only the content area slides.

The gesture engine automatically distinguishes horizontal swipes from vertical scrolling: once it detects you're swiping sideways, it locks scroll for a clean animation with no page drift. A short flick works too — you don't need to drag the full way across.

Swiping is intentionally ignored on inputs, buttons, the canvas, modals, and the reorder-drag handle.
Offline Mode
When no connection is available, the app automatically serves the last cached data and shows an ⚡ OFFLINE MODE banner with the cache age. Tap the banner to retry when back online.
📡 Data Sources & Decoding Reference
AVWX API — Primary Weather Source
All METAR, TAF, and station data is fetched from avwx.rest. Requests are load-balanced across multiple API keys and cached for 10 minutes to conserve quota. AVWX aggregates data from official national meteorological services worldwide.
aviationweather.gov — NOTAMs & Airport Data
NOTAMs and supplementary airport frequencies/phones are sourced from aviationweather.gov, the FAA/NOAA public API. NOTAM data is cached for 10 minutes. This is an official U.S. government source.
Open-Meteo — 24h Meteogram & Upper-Level Winds
The meteogram chart and upper-level wind data (925/850/700 hPa) use open-meteo.com, an open-source NWP model aggregator. This data is advisory only — it is a numerical weather prediction model, not an official aviation forecast.
Flight Rules (VFR / MVFR / IFR / LIFR)
Calculated using ICAO/FAA standard thresholds:
VFR — Ceiling > 3000 ft and Visibility > 5 sm
MVFR — Ceiling 1000–3000 ft or Vis 3–5 sm
IFR — Ceiling < 1000 ft or Vis < 3 sm
LIFR — Ceiling < 500 ft or Vis < 1 sm
Sky Cover Codes
SKC / CLR — Sky Clear (0 oktas)
FEW — Few clouds (1–2 oktas)
SCT — Scattered (3–4 oktas)
BKN — Broken — ceiling layer (5–7 oktas)
OVC — Overcast — ceiling layer (8 oktas)
VV — Vertical Visibility (sky obscured — ceiling layer)
Altitudes are reported in hundreds of feet AGL (e.g. BKN025 = 2500 ft).
Weather Phenomena Codes
Common codes: TS Thunderstorm · RA Rain · SN Snow · FG Fog · BR Mist · HZ Haze · DZ Drizzle · GR Hail · SQ Squall · FC Funnel Cloud
Intensity prefixes: − Light · (none) Moderate · + Heavy
Descriptors: SH Showers · FZ Freezing · BL Blowing · DR Drifting · MI Shallow · BC Patches
Wind Component Calculation
Headwind and crosswind are computed trigonometrically using the difference between the magnetic wind direction and the runway heading. Magnetic variation is sourced from the AVWX station record.
Headwind = Wind Speed × cos(Δ heading)
Crosswind = Wind Speed × sin(Δ heading)
ATC Audio — Taiwan (TWATC.net)
In-app live audio streams for RCTP, RCSS, RCKH, RCMQ, RCBS, RCFN are provided by TWATC.net, a Taiwan-based ATC audio streaming service. Streams include ATIS and area scanner feeds.
US Airport Resources (AirNav, iFlightPlanner, 1800wxbrief)
When a US airport is loaded, three additional resource links appear in the ATC tab under the External Resources section.

AirNav — airnav.com — Comprehensive airport directory with frequencies, fuel, services, and runway data for US airports.

iFlightPlanner — iflightplanner.com — Opens in-app with three tabs: Airport (info & diagram), VFR Sectional (interactive chart), and IFR Low (enroute chart centred on the airport).

1800wxbrief / FlightService — 1800wxbrief.com — Official FAA Flight Service Station briefing portal. Links directly to the airport info page for standard weather briefings and NOTAMs.

All three links update automatically when you search a different US airport.
ATC Audio — Global (LiveATC.net)
For all other airports, METAR GO links to LiveATC.net, which provides ATC audio streams for hundreds of airports worldwide. The button auto-searches by your current ICAO code.
⚠️ Disclaimer: All data is for reference only. Always verify with official sources before flight. METAR GO is not certified for operational use and does not replace official pre-flight briefings.
